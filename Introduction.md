

# **Introduction to Networking**

## **Computer Network**

A computer network is a collection of computing devices connected in different ways to communicate and share resources.

* Connections can be:

  * Physical wires or cables
  * Wireless (radio waves or infrared signals)

**Node / Host:** Any device connected to a network.

---

## **Data Transfer Rate**

The speed at which data moves from one place to another in a network.

---

## **Client–Server Model**

Computer networks support the client/server model.

* **File Server:** Stores and manages files for multiple users.
* **Web Server:** Responds to browser requests for web pages.

---

# **Types of Networks**

## **1. Local Area Network (LAN)**

Connects computers within a small geographical area.

### LAN Topologies

* Ring topology
* Star topology
* Bus topology

---

## **Ring Topology**

All nodes are connected in a closed loop.

* Messages travel in one direction.

---

## **Star Topology**

All devices connect to a central hub.

**Working:**
Device → Hub → Destination Device

**Advantages**

* Easy installation
* Less expensive
* Easy management

**Disadvantages**

* If hub fails, entire network stops.

---

## **Bus Topology**

All devices connect to a single backbone cable.

**Features**

* Uses drop lines and taps.
* Failure of one device can affect whole network.

---

## **2. Wide Area Network (WAN)**

Connects multiple LANs over large geographic distances.

* Uses gateways for communication.
* The Internet is the largest WAN.

---

## **3. Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)**

Network infrastructure covering large cities.

---

## **Gateway**

Connects LANs over long distances to form WAN.

---

## **Internet Backbone**

High-speed networks carrying internet traffic.
Example providers: AT&T, GTE, IBM.

---

## **Internet Service Provider (ISP)**

Company providing internet access to users.

---

# **Internet Connection Technologies**

* **Phone Modem:** Converts digital signals to analog.
* **DSL:** Uses telephone copper lines.
* **Cable Modem:** Uses cable TV lines.
* **Broadband:** Speed greater than 128 bps.

Download speed ≠ Upload speed.

---

# **Network Devices**

## **Hub**

* Connects computers in star topology.
* Works at Physical Layer.
* Sends data to all ports.

---

## **Repeater**

* Receives and regenerates signals.
* Extends transmission distance.

---

## **Modem**

Modulator + Demodulator
Converts digital signals to analog and back.

Measured in **bps (bits per second).**

---

## **NIC (Network Interface Card)**

Hardware that allows computer network communication.

* Works on OSI Layer 1 & 2
* Uses MAC Address

---

## **Switch**

* Intelligent device.
* Sends data only to required port.
* Uses MAC address table.

---

## **Bridge**

* Operates at Data Link Layer.
* Connects network segments.
* Uses MAC address database.

---

## **Router**

* Operates at Network Layer.
* Routes data between networks.
* Understands IP addressing.

---

## **Wireless Access Point (WAP)**

Allows wireless devices to connect using Wi-Fi or Bluetooth.

---

# **Transmission Media**

## **Guided Media**

1. Twisted Pair Cable
2. Coaxial Cable
3. Optical Fiber Cable

## **Unguided Media**

1. Radio Waves
2. Microwaves
3. Infrared

---

## **Twisted Pair Cable**

Two wires twisted together.

**Advantages**

* Cheap
* Easy installation

**Disadvantages**

* Low speed
* Short distance

---

## **Coaxial Cable**

Shielded copper cable.

**Advantages**

* High bandwidth
* Less interference
* High frequency support

**Disadvantages**

* Bulky
* Expensive installation
* Grounding required

---

## **Optical Fiber Cable**

Transfers data using light signals.

**Types**

* Single Mode Fiber
* Multi Mode Fiber

**Advantages**

* Very high bandwidth
* Long distance
* EMI resistant
* Secure

**Disadvantages**

* Expensive
* Difficult installation
* Fragile

---

# **Switching Techniques**

## **1. Circuit Switching**

Dedicated path established before transmission.

**Advantages**

* Guaranteed data rate
* No delay during transmission

**Disadvantages**

* High bandwidth usage
* Setup time required

---

## **2. Packet Switching**

Message divided into packets and sent separately.

**Advantages**

* Efficient network usage
* No dedicated path
* Lower cost

**Disadvantages**

* Packet loss
* Delay
* Order mismatch

---

## **3. Message Switching**

Entire message sent and stored at each node (Store-and-Forward).

**Advantages**

* Channel sharing
* Unlimited message size

**Disadvantages**

* Not suitable for voice/video
* Costly
* Less reliable
